 About CHED   

The Center for Health and Environmental Data strives to provide timely, innovative and high-quality public health data collection, analysis and reporting services to drive evidence-based decisions, translate data into action and support policy development to protect and improve the health and environment of all Coloradans.

CoHID (Colorado Health Information Dataset)

The NEW Colorado Health Information Dataset (CoHID) site provides improved access to state and local-level data and resources compiled by the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment and its partners to help understand health and related issues affecting people in Colorado. On CoHID you will find information about:

  • Live birth data.
  • Death data.
  • Cancer incidence data.
  • Birth defects data.
  • Suicide data.
  • Drug overdose data.
  • Child fatality.
  • Population estimates.
  • Chronic disease data.
  • Maternal health data.
  • Colorado health indicators.
  • Adolescent and student health data.
  • Environmental epidemiology data.
  • Communicable disease and STI/HIV data.
  • Immunization data.
  • Marijuana and health data.
  • Community health equity maps.
  • Community inclusion and resource maps.
  • CDPHE open data.
 
This new COHID site improves upon its long-standing predecessor, continuing to provide data collected through a variety of CHED’s public health surveillance programs and its public health partners:
  • Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS).
  • Colorado Central Cancer Registry.
  • Colorado Responds to Children With Special Needs.
  • Colorado Violent Death Reporting System.
  • Colorado Vital Statistics Program.
  • Healthy Kids Colorado Survey (HKCS).
  • Pregnancy Risk Assessment Monitoring System (PRAMS).
  • Colorado Child Fatality Prevention System.
  • Colorado Immunization Information Service (CIIS).
  • Colorado Opioid Overdose Prevention Program.
  • Environmental Public Health Tracking Network Program (EPHTN).
  • Retail Marijuana Health Monitoring Program.
  • Demography Office, Colorado Department of Local Affairs.
  • Communicable Disease and STI/HIV/Vital Hepatitis Branches, CDPHE.

Public Health Informatics

Explore data

  • Data source factsheets.
  • VISION (Visual Information System for Identifying Opportunities and Needs. Chronic disease and behavioral health measures. Prioritized and presented with interactive data visualizations).
  • Colorado health indicators (Interactive dashboard for exploring multiple indicators, including county-, regional- and state-level data on a variety of health, environmental and social topics).
  • CoHiD (Colorado Health Information Dataset).
  • Colorado suicide data dashboard.
  • Healthy Kids Colorado Survey data dashboard.
  • Health maps and GIS.
  • Colorado BMI monitoring project (Community-level overweight and obesity prevalence estimates).
  • Community level estimates (Community-level estimates for important health conditions and risk behavior indicators).
  • Community health equity map (A platform for visualizing geographic disparities for selected social determinants of health and key health conditions/outcomes across Colorado).
  • Community inclusion resources and maps (Easy-to-access, location-based community information for emergency preparedness and response planning).
  • CDPHE open data (Selected geospatial data sets, maps, visualization tools, and web-based mapping applications that are published by CDPHE).
