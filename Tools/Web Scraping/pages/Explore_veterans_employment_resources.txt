Veterans Receive Employment Assistance
All veterans receive
Priority of Service
in all of Colorado's Workforce Center services, and have the opportunity to work with the highly skilled, professional Workforce Center staff to find a job that maximizes their potential!
How We Can Help!
Colorado Workforce Center staff and Veterans Employment Specialists are available to serve veterans by providing expertise in job and training opportunities. Our partnerships with local Workforce Centers allow any veteran who has served at least one day of active duty to receive additional support with their job search. Workforce Center staff or an Employment Specialists can provide "Intensive" services to veterans and eligible spouses facing significant barriers to employment.
We're available at Workforce Center locations (view Workforce Center Map below), Veteran Employment Specialists provide one-on-one assistance to eligible veterans and spouses seeking guidance in obtaining employment.
Our team is dedicated to helping you understand and align your skill-sets with available opportunities across the state. Our goal is to provide you with the information necessary to make confident career choices. All of this at no cost to you!
Veterans' Priority of Service
#FFFFFF
SERVICES WE PROVIDE
ONE ON ONE SERVICES
Let us be your one-on-one career coach. As fellow veterans, we know how to help. Our specialists know firsthand what businesses are looking for in a résumé, and the questions employers are likely to ask in an interview. We'll guide you through:
Résumé and cover letter writing
Interview tips and techniques
Translating military experience to civilian responsibilities
Identifying education, training and experience requirements
Navigating online job boards and career fair resources
Targeting your job search by industry, and more
WORKFORCE CENTER SERVICES
In addition to assistance from one of our specialists, be sure to take advantage of our Colorado Workforce Center Services:
Workshops for resume preparation, interviewing techniques and job search
Training and grant programs like on-the-job training and more
Information on wage rates and employment trends
Vocational guidance and career counseling
Referrals to services within the community
Access to phone, internet, printer, fax and copy machines
