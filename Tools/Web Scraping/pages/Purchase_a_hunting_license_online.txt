2024 CPW Product Sales Chart for Customer Purchases
- provides a high level overview of on sale dates, license application deadlines and valid to-from dates, as applicable.
Things to know when buying online or by phone
The 2024 annual license year runs 3/1/2024 - 3/31/2025 (13 months).
Over-the-counter unlimited spring turkey licenses go on sale March 1, 2024 and annual mountain lion licenses March 25, 2024.
Customers may use Visa, MasterCard or Discover credit cards. Visa, MasterCard​, or Discover debit cards work if they can be used without a PIN. Licenses are mailed the business day following the purchase.​
Customers must complete all requirements for
Hunter Education
before they apply for or buy a hunting license or before they apply for a preference point.
You must have your physical license to hunt any animal that requires a carcass tag such as big game and turkey, the TAN does not apply.
Items sent via standard shipping may take 30 days to arrive from your order date. If you need your product sooner, you need to select "pick-up at sales location" or pay the extra fee for express shipping.
Except for big game, turkey licenses, and the Colorado Waterfowl Stamp, a temporary authorization number (TAN) will be provided for the purchased license(s). The TAN is currently valid for 45 days after the date of purchase. Please print or write down (must be legible) the TAN and carry it with you while hunting or fishing until you receive your license. If the license OR all of the licenses you purchase in this transaction expire in less than 15 ​days, you will NOT receive anything in the mail.
If there are any issues, please contact the call center at (303) 297-1192 or a CPW park or office.
Telephone sales representatives at 1-800-244-5613 are trained to sell licenses and do not know specific wildlife-related information for Colorado. For questions not related to the sale of licenses, call Colorado Parks and Wildlife at (303) 297-1192.

​Mule deer by the number: How CPW calculates wildlife population​
​CPW has been inventorying deer populations, as well as other species such as elk, from the air for over 50 years. The​ aerial inventory helps CPW understand fawn reproductive rates and set wildlife population goals as well as the number of hunting licenses offered every year.
